# West Africa Geopolitical Economics

## Key Figure: Romuald Wadagni — President-Elect of Benin

*Research compiled April 14, 2026 | Sources: Al Jazeera, Reuters, BBC, Wikipedia, Ujasusi Intelligence, AFP, Le Monde, Democracy in Africa*

---

## 1. Biography

**Full Name**: Romuald Kossi Wadagni
**Born**: June 20, 1976 (age 49), in Lokossa, Benin
**Family**: Eldest of five children. His father, Nestor Wadagni, was a statistician and economist trained at ENSAE who served in the Beninese senior civil service.

### Education
- **Master's in Finance, École Supérieure des Affaires (ESA) de Grenoble, France** (1995–1999) — graduated top of class
- **U.S. Certified Public Accountant (CPA)** — obtained 2003
- **Harvard Business School** — Private Equity & Venture Capital program (2007); General Management Program (2015)

### Professional Career (17 years at Deloitte)
- Joined Deloitte Lyon in **1998**
- Transferred to **Boston** as Audit Manager
- **Deloitte's youngest-ever partner** in 2012 at age 36
- **Founded Deloitte DRC** as CEO from 2012
- **Head of Audit, Francophone Africa** from November 2015
- Left Deloitte in 2016 for government service

---

## 2. Political Career

### Minister of Economy and Finance (2016–2026)
Appointed by President Patrice Talon in April 2016 — his first political role.

**Key Accomplishments:**
- **GDP growth**: Benin achieved **7% GDP growth in 2025**, among highest in West Africa
- **Budget expansion**: National budget **tripled** over his tenure
- **IMF agreement**: Secured **$700 million IMF facility** in 2022
- **Eurobond milestones**: First **US dollar bond ($750M)** in 2024; **first African country** to issue a Eurobond for UN SDGs (2021)
- **Credit ratings**: Maintained **investment-grade** from Fitch and Moody's
- **Business environment**: Ranked **#1 globally** for ease of starting a business (UNCTAD 2020)
- **CFA franc reform**: As Chairman of WAEMU Finance Ministers' Council (2018–2020), led negotiations for **phasing out the CFA franc** — described as historic by African Development Bank

### Coalition Candidacy
On **August 31, 2025**, the ruling coalition (UPR + Bloc Républicain) designated Wadagni as presidential candidate **without primaries**.

## 4. Political Context

### Benin's Political System
- **Presidential republic** with **two-round electoral system**
- **7-year term** (changed from 5 by 2025 amendment)
- **Two-term limit**, even if non-consecutive
- Talon respected term limits — notable contrast to regional trends

### The December 2025 Coup Attempt
- **December 7, 2025**: Military elements attempted to overthrow Talon
- **France** provided surveillance/logistical support; **Nigerian aircraft** bombed rebel camp
- **ECOWAS** deployed stabilization forces
- **Plot leaders fled**, some reportedly hiding in Togo
- Part of broader **Sahel coup wave** (Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger since 2020)

### Democratic Concerns
- **No primaries**: Wadagni handpicked by Talon
- **Opposition exclusion**: Multiple candidates barred
- **94% margin**: Questions about genuine competition
- Despite concerns, **peaceful constitutional transfer** remains significant regionally

## 6. Regional Significance

### ECOWAS & Sahel Crisis
- After **Mali, Burkura Faso, and Niger** exited ECOWAS (forming Alliance of Sahel States), Benin is a key **pro-Western coastal democracy**
- **Northern border**: Active **jihadist insurgency zone since 2021** (al-Qaeda-linked JNIM, Islamic State Sahel)
- **Niger relations**: Wadagni pledged economic leverage via **Port of Cotonou** — Niger's primary trade corridor
- **WAEMU leadership**: Central role in **CFA franc reform**

---

## Assessment

Wadagni represents a **technocratic, pro-Western continuity candidate** central to Benin's decade of reform. His Harvard/Deloitte credentials and 10-year finance record give strong international credibility. His presidency will likely maintain Benin's Western alignment while navigating **northern jihadist insecurity** and **regional diplomatic isolation** following the Sahel coups.

Principal concern: **democratic legitimacy** of 94% victory with opposition exclusion, and whether Talon continues to wield influence behind the scenes.
