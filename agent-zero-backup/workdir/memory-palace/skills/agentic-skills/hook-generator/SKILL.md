# Hook Generator

## When to Use
Activate when asked to create hooks, openers, first lines, or attention-grabbing starts for any content type — tweets, videos, emails, articles, ads.

## Hook Types

### 1. The Bold Claim
Make a surprising statement that demands attention.
> "Most of what you know about [topic] is wrong."

### 2. The Curiosity Gap
Tease knowledge without revealing it.
> "I discovered a trick that doubled my output in 2 weeks. Here's what it is."

### 3. The Contrarian
Challenge common wisdom.
> "Everyone says you need [X]. They're wrong."

### 4. The Story Opener
Start mid-narrative.
> "Last Tuesday, I almost quit. Here's why I didn't."

### 5. The Stat Shock
Lead with a surprising number.
> "97% of people get this wrong."

### 6. The Direct Address
Speak personally to the reader.
> "If you're struggling with [X], stop everything and read this."

### 7. The List Tease
Preview value without details.
> "7 tools that replaced my entire workflow. Number 4 is free."

## The Process

### Step 1: Understand Context
- What's the content about?
- What emotion should the hook create?
- What platform? (affects length)

### Step 2: Generate Options (minimum 5)
For each hook, provide:
- The hook text
- Type (from list above)
- Why it works (psychological mechanism)
- Best for (platform/format)

### Step 3: Rank and Recommend
- Rank by predicted engagement
- Select the top 3 for the specific context
- Explain why they win

```markdown

**Type:** [Type] | **Best for:** [Platform]

### Third Pick

4. [Hook] — [Type]

6. [Hook] — [Type]

```

## Constraints
- Hooks must work in under 3 seconds of reading
- No clickbait — the content must deliver on the hook's promise
- Adapt length to platform (280 chars for X, 5 sec for video, etc.)
- Generate at least 7 options per request
