# Knowledge Structuring Skill

## When to Use
Activate when asked to organize, structure, or make sense of messy notes, scattered information, or unstructured research data.

## The Process

### Step 1: Ingest Raw Material
- Collect all notes, documents, and data points
- Tag each item with its source and type
- Identify duplicate or redundant entries

### Step 2: Identify Themes
- Group related items by topic
- Find natural clusters and hierarchies
- Note cross-cutting themes
- Identify orphans (items that don't fit neatly)

### Step 3: Build the Hierarchy

```markdown
# [Knowledge Domain]

## [Category 1]
### [Sub-category 1a]
- [Item/concept]
- [Item/concept]
### [Sub-category 1b]
- [Item/concept]

## [Category 2]
### [Sub-category 2a]
- [Item/concept]

## Cross-Cutting Themes
- [Theme that spans categories]

## Open Questions
- [Things that need further research]

## Orphan Items
- [Items that don't fit the current structure]
```

### Step 4: Apply a Framework (choose best fit)
- **MECE** (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive)
- **Chronological** (Timeline-based)
- **Spatial** (Geography/location-based)
- **Taxonomic** (Classification hierarchy)
- **Funnel** (Broad to specific)
- **Matrix** (Multi-dimensional)

## Constraints
- Every item must have a home (even if it's "orphan")
- Hierarchy should go max 4 levels deep
- Cross-references are encouraged between categories
- The structure should make the information easier to navigate, not harder
